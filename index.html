Woodside Residents Push Back Against the Tide of Displacement

Historically a quiet residential neighborhood in the heart of Queens, Woodside has increasingly attracted the attention of developers.

NOVEMBER 20, 2023 · By Kimberly Izar


QUEENS, NY – Woodside residents are pushing back on what could be a lengthy battle with luxury real estate developers in their neighborhood.

Last year, Madison Capital Realty began leasing at Woodside Central, a 478-unit, two story tower on 69th and 70th streets in Woodside. But advocates worry this will increase the surrounding area’s rent and cost of living expenses.

Based on available listings on the Woodside Central website, the median rent for a 2-bedroom unit in the luxury building is over 40% higher than the median rent for a similar sized unit in the neighborhood.



Maria Seguera is one Woodside resident that is concerned about looming rent hikes and its impacts.

“My income as a home attendant is extremely limited and it barely allows me to survive with the high cost of living,” said Maria Seguera, a single mother of two daughters, at a city rent guidelines board meeting earlier this June.

“It’s very sad and embarrassing to come here every year in front of you guys and beg for something that’s our right.”
